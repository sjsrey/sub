Heat Map Gone Wild

Date Sun 10 August 2014 Tags visualization

I stumbled on this rather striking example of cartography gone wild:

(Source: Wall Street OTC)

So many things a cartographer could say about this. For starters, heat maps are typically used with raster data and the state map depicted here uses a vector representation. Beyond that, the color scheme induces a good bout of cognitive dissonance in the reader. A heat map implies that hotter colors are associated with higher attribute values (see an example here). But think of the viewer, what color would be interpreted as hotter, red or green? Yeah, the red states of course. Actually, no, the green states (green as in go fast, I guess?) have higher broadband speeds.

Ouch.
